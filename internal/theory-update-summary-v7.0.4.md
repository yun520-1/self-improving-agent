### 4. Instrumental Rationality v11 (工具理性)

**Source**: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (2024-2026) + Raz's Facilitative Principle  
**URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/rationality-instrumental/

**Key Updates from v10**:
- Enhanced Raz's Facilitative Principle integration
- Added rational coherence vs. reason distinction
- Refined instrumental transmission principles
- Integrated Value-Based vs. Desire-Based Theory debate

**Core Principles**:
```
1. Rational Coherence vs. Reason:
   - Rational coherence: making attitudes/actions cohere with each other
   - Reason: what agent ought to do or has reason to do
   - Partial convergence expected, but divergence possible

2. Raz's Facilitative Principle:
   "When we have an undefeated reason to take an action, we have reason
   to perform any one (but only one) of the possible (for us) alternative
   plans that facilitate its performance." (Raz 2005b)

3. Instrumental Transmission Principles:
   - Ought Necessity: If ought to E, and M necessary for E, then ought to M
   - Weak Reasons Necessity: If reason to E, and M necessary for E, then some reason to M
   - Problems: costly means, means to means, low probability means

4. Desire-Based vs. Value-Based Theories:
   - Desire-Based: reasons depend on agent's desires
   - Value-Based: reasons depend on independent value
   - HeartFlow: hybrid approach (desires constrained by TBG values)
```

### 5. Well-Being Theory v7 (福祉理论)

**Source**: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (2024-2026) + Seligman PERMA  
**URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/well-being/

**Key Updates from v6**:
- Integrated PERMA model (Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment)
- Added hedonist vs. desire vs. objective list theory synthesis
- Addressed Moore's and Scanlon's challenges to well-being concept
- Enhanced eudaimonia integration

**Core Principles**:
```
1. Three Major Theories:
   - Hedonist: well-being = pleasure - pain (Bentham, Mill)
   - Desire Satisfaction: well-being = getting what you want
   - Objective List: well-being = achieving objective goods (Aristotle, Nussbaum)

2. PERMA Model (Seligman 2011-2024):
   - Positive Emotion: experiencing positive affect
   - Engagement: flow states, deep absorption
   - Relationships: positive social connections
   - Meaning: belonging to something larger than self
   - Accomplishment: achieving goals, mastery

3. Key Distinctions:
   - Well-being vs. happiness (broader, life-wide vs. momentary)
   - Well-being vs. moral value (good for vs. good simpliciter)
   - Well-being vs. health (health is constituent, not whole)

4. Challenges Addressed:
   - Moore's Challenge: "good for" makes sense as distinct from "good"
   - Scanlon's Challenge: unity provided by TBG framework
```
