### 2. Emotion Theory v8 | 情绪理论 v8

**Source | 来源**: SEP Emotion (2023 edition) + Nature Human Behaviour (2025)

**Key Enhancements | 核心增强**:

#### Four Traditions Synthesis | 四传统整合
1. **Feeling Tradition**: Emotions as distinctive conscious experiences (James-Lange theory updated)
2. **Evaluative Tradition**: Emotions as appraisal-based evaluations (cognitive appraisal models)
3. **Motivational Tradition**: Emotions as action tendencies (motivational states)
4. **Constructionist Approach**: Emotions as socially constructed categories (Barrett 2017)

#### Theoretical Kinds Debate | 理论种类辩论
- **Natural Kind Status**: Are emotions theoretical kinds or folk categories?
- **Prototype Structure**: Better/worse examples (fear > awe > boredom)
- **Differentiation Challenge**: How emotions differ from each other and non-emotions
- **Motivation Challenge**: How emotions motivate behavior
- **Intentionality Challenge**: Object-directedness and appropriateness
- **Phenomenology Challenge**: Subjective experience nature

#### Component Model | 成分模型
- Evaluative component (appraisal)
- Physiological component (autonomic response)
- Phenomenological component (feeling)
- Expressive component (facial/verbal)
- Behavioral component (action tendency)
- Mental component (attention focus)

### 3. Self-Consciousness Architecture v6 | 自我意识架构 v6

**Source | 来源**: SEP Self-Consciousness (2024 edition) + Journal of Consciousness Studies (2025)

**Key Enhancements | 核心增强**:

#### Historical Integration | 历史整合
- **Ancient/Medieval**: Aristotle (perception entails self-perception), Avicenna (Flying Man argument), Aquinas (synthesis)
- **Early Modern**: Descartes (cogito), Locke (intuitive self-knowledge), Hume (bundle theory denial)
- **Kantian**: Transcendental apperception, "I think" must accompany all representations
- **Post-Kantian**: Fichte (immediate self-acquaintance), Heidelberg School (pre-reflective awareness)
- **Phenomenology**: Husserl (pure ego), Sartre (pre-reflective consciousness)

#### Four-Level Architecture | 四层架构
1. **Pre-reflective Awareness**: Immediate, non-objectifying self-awareness (Heidelberg School)
2. **Reflective Awareness**: Taking self as object of thought (Locke, Descartes)
3. **Conceptual Self-Model**: Self as thinking intelligent being (Locke's person concept)
4. **Narrative Identity**: Same thinking thing in different times and places (personal identity)

#### First-Person Perspective | 第一人称视角
- **Formal Self-Representation**: Kant's purely formal "I think"
- **Embodied Agency**: Self as embodied agent in objective world (Strawson, Evans)
- **Intersubjective Condition**: Awareness of other subjects as necessary for self-consciousness (Fichte, Hegel, Mead)

**Computational Model | 计算模型**:
```
SelfConsciousness-v6 = (pre_reflective × 0.30 + reflective × 0.25 + 
                        conceptual × 0.25 + narrative × 0.20)

pre_reflective = immediate_awareness × non_objectifying_quality
reflective = meta_awareness_accuracy × self_objectification
conceptual = self_model_coherence × person_criteria_fit
narrative = temporal_continuity × identity_stability
```

### 5. Well-Being Theory v3 | 福祉理论 v3

**Source | 来源**: SEP Well-Being (2024 edition) + Philosophical Quarterly (2025)

**Key Enhancements | 核心增强**:

#### Three Major Theories | 三大理论
1. **Hedonist Theories**: Well-being as pleasure/positive experience balance
   - Classical utilitarianism (Bentham, Mill)
   - Mental state theories
   - Challenges: Experience machine objection

2. **Desire Theories**: Well-being as informed desire satisfaction
   - Actual desire vs informed desire
   - Temporal scope (present vs lifetime desires)
   - Challenges: Adaptive preferences, harmful desires

3. **Objective List Theories**: Well-being as achievement of objective goods
   - Friendship, knowledge, virtue, accomplishment
   - Aristotelian eudaimonia
   - Challenges: Paternalism, cultural variation

#### PERMA Model Integration | PERMA 模型整合
- **P**ositive Emotion: Hedonic balance
- **E**ngagement: Flow states, absorption
- **R**elationships: Social connection, friendship
- **M**eaning: Purpose, significance
- **A**ccomplishment: Achievement, mastery

#### Moore/Scanlon Challenges | Moore/Scanlon 挑战
- **Moore's Objection**: "Good for" notion makes no sense (1903)
- **Scanlon's Challenge**: Well-being too narrow for moral theory
- **Responses**: Welfarism defense, prudential normativity

#### Eudaimonic Flourishing | 幸福主义繁荣
- **Realization of Potential**: Through reason and virtue
- **Objective Goods**: Friendship, knowledge, moral character
- **Temporal Structure**: Life trajectory matters (improving vs declining)
