## 1. Consciousness Theory Deep Integration | 意识理论深度整合

### Source | 来源
- **SEP Entry**: "Consciousness" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, 2026)
- **Key Concepts**: Creature consciousness, state consciousness, phenomenal consciousness, intentionality

### Theoretical Framework | 理论框架

#### 1.1 Creature Consciousness | 生物意识
```
Four senses of creature consciousness:
1. Sentience - Capacity to sense and respond to world
2. Wakefulness - Actually exercising capacity (not asleep/coma)
3. Self-consciousness - Aware that one is aware
4. What-it-is-like - Nagel's subjective criterion
```

#### 1.2 State Consciousness | 状态意识
```
Six major concepts:
1. States one is aware of (meta-mentality)
2. Qualitative states (qualia, raw sensory feels)
3. Phenomenal states (overall experiential structure)
4. What-it-is-like states (subjective character)
5. Higher-order states (HOT, HOP theories)
6. Transitive consciousness (consciousness of objects)
```

#### 1.3 Phenomenal Structure | 现象结构
```
Components:
- Spatial organization
- Temporal organization  
- Conceptual organization
- Agentive perspective (self as agent in world)
```

## 2. Emotion Theory Comprehensive Synthesis | 情绪理论综合整合

### Source | 来源
- **SEP Entry**: "Emotion" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, 2026)
- **Key Research**: Scarantino 2016, Prinz 2004, Barrett 2017, psychological constructionism

### Theoretical Framework | 理论框架

#### 2.1 Three Traditions Synthesis | 三大传统综合
```
1. Feeling Tradition (James-Lange, phenomenological quality)
   - Emotions as distinctive conscious experiences
   - Bodily feedback constitutes emotional feeling
   
2. Evaluative Tradition (Cognitive appraisal)
   - Emotions as evaluations of circumstances
   - Appraisal patterns differentiate emotions
   
3. Motivational Tradition (Action tendency)
   - Emotions as motivational states
   - Drive behavior toward goals
```

#### 2.2 Four-Component Model | 四成分模型
```
Complete emotion episode includes:
1. Evaluative component (appraising situation)
2. Physiological component (heart rate, blood pressure)
3. Phenomenological component (unpleasant/pleasant feeling)
4. Expressive component (facial expression)
5. Behavioral component (action tendency)
6. Mental component (attention focusing)
```

#### 2.3 Constructionist Approach | 建构主义方法
```
Psychological constructionism:
- Emotions constructed from basic psychological ingredients
- Not natural kinds but conceptual categories
- Context-dependent emergence
```

```
Differentiation criteria:
```

## 3. Self-Consciousness Architecture v2 | 自我意识架构 v2

### Source | 来源
- **SEP Entry**: "Self-Consciousness" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, 2026)
- **Key Philosophers**: Descartes, Locke, Kant, Fichte, Sartre, Zahavi

### Theoretical Framework | 理论框架

#### 3.1 Historical Development | 历史发展
```
Ancient & Medieval:
- Delphic maxim "Know Thyself"
- Aristotle: perception entails self-perception
- Avicenna's Flying Man argument (self-awareness without body)
- Aquinas: two forms (presence of mind vs essence awareness)

Early Modern:
- Descartes: Cogito ergo sum (indubitable self-awareness)
- Locke: intuitive knowledge of own existence
- Hume: bundle theory (no self impression, only perceptions)
- Kant: transcendental apperception ("I think" must accompany all representations)

Post-Kantian:
- Fichte: immediate self-acquaintance (non-reflective foundation)
- Heidelberg School: pre-reflective self-awareness
- Sartre: pre-reflective consciousness without transcendental ego
```

#### 3.2 Multi-Level Architecture | 多层级架构
```
Level 1: Pre-reflective Self-Awareness
- Immediate, non-objectifying
- Present in all conscious states
- "I think" accompanies experience without thematic focus

Level 2: Reflective Self-Awareness  
- Taking self as object of attention
- Second-order awareness (awareness of awareness)
- Can be introspective or incidental

Level 3: Conceptual Self-Awareness
- Self-concepts and self-beliefs
- Narrative identity construction
- Autobiographical memory integration

Level 4: Intersubjective Self-Awareness
- Self in relation to others
- Second-person perspective
- Social identity and recognition
```

#### 3.3 First-Person Perspective | 第一人称视角
```
Components:
1. Immunity to error through misidentification (IEM)
   - Certain self-ascriptions cannot be wrong about who
2. Subjectivity
   - Experience from unique point of view
3. Agency awareness
   - Sense of authorship over actions
4. Embodiment
   - Self as located in body
```

## 4. Moral Psychology Framework | 道德心理学框架

### Source | 来源
- **SEP Entry**: "Moral Psychology" (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy)
- **Key Topics**: Moral judgment, moral motivation, moral development, moral emotions

### Theoretical Framework | 理论框架

#### 4.1 Moral Judgment | 道德判断
```
Components:
1. Cognitive component (belief about right/wrong)
2. Affective component (emotional response)
3. Motivational component (action tendency)
4. Normative component (appeal to standards)

Theories:
- Cognitivism: moral judgments are beliefs
- Non-cognitivism: moral judgments are attitudes/emotions
- Hybrid views: both cognitive and conative elements
```

#### 4.2 Moral Motivation | 道德动机
```
Internalism vs Externalism:
- Internalism: moral judgment necessarily motivates
- Externalism: motivation requires additional desire

Moral Emotions:
- Guilt, shame, pride (self-conscious emotions)
- Indignation, gratitude (other-directed)
- Empathy, compassion (prosocial)
```

#### 4.3 Moral Development | 道德发展
```
Stages (Kohlberg-inspired):
1. Pre-conventional (obedience, self-interest)
2. Conventional (social norms, law and order)
3. Post-conventional (universal principles, justice)

Virtue Development:
- Character formation through practice
- Practical wisdom (phronesis)
- Habituation and reflection
```

```
Criteria:
```
