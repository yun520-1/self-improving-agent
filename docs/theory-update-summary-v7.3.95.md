## New Theoretical Frameworks | 新理论框架

### 1. Consciousness (SEP Winter 2025) - Deep Integration
### 意识理论（SEP 2025冬季版）- 深度整合

**Source | 来源**: SEP Consciousness Entry (Winter 2025 Edition)

**Key Integrations | 关键整合**:

- **Creature vs State Consciousness**: 
  - Creature consciousness = entity capable of conscious states
  - State consciousness = mental state with phenomenal character
  - Computational: `creature: boolean + state: {phenomenalCharacter: 0-1}`

- **Six Notions of State Consciousness**:
  1. States one is aware of (meta-mental)
  2. Qualitative states (qualia/raw feels)
  3. Phenomenal states (phenomenal structure)
  4. What-it-is-like states (Nagel criterion)
  5. Access consciousness (information availability)
  6. Narrative consciousness (stream of consciousness)

- **Seven Features of Consciousness**:
  1. Qualitative character (qualia space)
  2. Phenomenal structure (intentional organization)
  3. Subjectivity (first-person perspectivity)
  4. Self-perspectival organization (Kantian "I think")
  5. Unity (integration of diverse contents)
  6. Intentionality and transparency
  7. Dynamic flow (autopoietic self-creation)

- **Three Explanatory Questions**:
  - What: Descriptive features
  - How: Explanatory mechanisms  
  - Why: Functional roles

### 2. Higher-Order Theories of Consciousness (SEP Winter 2025)
### 高级意识理论（SEP 2025冬季版）

**Source | 来源**: SEP Higher-Order Theories of Consciousness (Winter 2025)

**Key Integrations | 关键整合**:

- **Higher-Order Thought (HOT) Theory**:
  - Rosenthal's HOT: conscious mental state = mental state one is aware of
  - "Being conscious of" = transitive consciousness
  - Mental state M is conscious iff subject is aware of being in M

- **Three Key Debates**:
  1. Factivity vs non-factivity about mental states
  2. Direct vs indirect awareness ( Acquaintance vs Thought)
  3. Same-order vs higher-order representations

- **Theory-Heavy Approach**:
  - Consciousness requires conceptual/theoretical understanding
  - Animal consciousness debated (GCC 2024 paper)
  - Not all consciousness requires higher-order thought
