### 1. Embodied Cognition (具身认知) v1

**Source**: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy  
**URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/embodied-cognition/

**Key Concepts**:
- Cognition is not merely computational symbol manipulation in the brain
- Body and environment play **constitutive** (not just causal) roles in cognitive processes
- Challenges traditional computational cognitive science assumptions (Chomsky, Newell, Simon)
- Aligns with ecological psychology (J.J. Gibson) and connectionist approaches
- Perception-action loops ground conceptual thought

**Core Principles**:
```
1. Cognition extends beyond neural computation
2. Body-environment coupling is constitutive of mental processes
3. Perceptual symbols ground abstract concepts (Wilson-Mendenhall et al. 2011)
4. Rejects strict mind-body dualism
5. Emphasizes dynamic interaction over static representation
```
