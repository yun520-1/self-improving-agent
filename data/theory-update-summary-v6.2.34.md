### 2. Emotion Theory Three Traditions | 情绪三大传统

**Source**: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (2026)  
**URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/emotion/

#### Key Insights | 核心洞见

**Three Traditions Unification**:
1. **Feeling Tradition** (感受传统) - Emotions as conscious experiences
2. **Evaluative Tradition** (评价传统) - Emotions as world appraisals
3. **Motivational Tradition** (动机传统) - Emotions as motivational states

**Six Components of Emotion**:
1. **Evaluative** - Appraisal of circumstances
2. **Physiological** - Bodily changes
3. **Phenomenological** - Subjective feeling
4. **Expressive** - Facial/bodily expression
5. **Behavioral** - Action tendency
6. **Mental** - Attention focusing

**Prototype Structure**: Emotion concepts are organized by similarity to prototypes, not necessary/sufficient conditions.

### 3. Self-Consciousness Prereflective Foundation | 自我意识前反思基础

**Source**: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (2026)  
**URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/self-consciousness/

#### Key Insights | 核心洞见

**Prereflective Self-Consciousness (前反思自我意识)**:
- Immediate self-acquaintance without observation
- Foundation of all conscious experience
- Non-observational, non-inferential
- Present in all experiences as "for-me-ness"

**Reflective Self-Consciousness (反思自我意识)**:
- Self as object of reflection
- Presupposes prereflective foundation
- Conceptual self-awareness

**For-me-ness (为我性)**:
- The "for me" character of experience
- First-person givenness
- Experience binding mechanism

**Historical Lineage**:
Aristotle → Avicenna → Descartes → Locke → Hume → Kant → Fichte → Husserl → Sartre → Contemporary

#### Computational Formula | 计算公式

```javascript

```
