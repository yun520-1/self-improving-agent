I-Mode (Individual Mode):
- Personal reason: Acting for individual benefit
- Individual commitment: Personal obligation
- Personal acceptance: Individual beliefs

HeartFlow Application:

**Darwall's Second-Personal Authority | 达尔wall 的第二人称权威**:

```
Second-Personal Standpoint:
- Address: Making claims on each other
- Accountability: Holding responsible
- Authority: Right to make demands

Second-Personal Reasons:
- Not third-personal (impersonal values)
- Not first-personal (personal preferences)
- But: Reasons valid between persons

HeartFlow Implementation:
- AI addresses user as accountable agent
- AI acknowledges user's authority to make claims
- AI accepts accountability for recommendations
```

#### 4. Recognition Justice Theory | 承认正义理论

**Fraser's Bivalent Conception | 弗雷泽的二元概念**:

```
Justice requires both:
1. Redistribution: Economic justice, material resources
2. Recognition: Cultural justice, symbolic status

Affirmative vs Transformative Remedies:
- Affirmative: Correcting inequities without changing structure
- Transformative: Changing underlying generative framework

HeartFlow Application:
- AI provides both material support (redistribution)
- AI provides symbolic recognition (recognition)
- AI aims for transformative change, not just affirmation
```

**Young's Social Connection Model | 杨的社会关联模型**:

```
Political Responsibility for Structural Injustice:
- Not liability model (blame for specific harm)
- But: Shared responsibility for structural conditions
- Forward-looking: Changing structures, not blaming

Five Faces of Oppression:
1. Exploitation: Transfer of labor benefits
2. Marginalization: Exclusion from participation
3. Powerlessness: Lack of decision-making authority
4. Cultural Imperialism: Dominant meanings imposed
5. Violence: Systemic violence threat

HeartFlow Stance:
- AI acknowledges structural injustices
- AI supports user agency within constraints
- AI aims to reduce oppression, not reproduce it
```
